    Section Reflection and Self-Assessment

    Checkpoint 7. Part 1: Matching Concepts to Strands.

    For each concept below, identify which strand (A, B, C, D, or E) it primarily belongs to. Write the letter of the strand in the space provided.
    _____ 1. Understanding that all data contains some level of bias
    _____ 2. Creating effective graphs that clearly communicate findings to an audience
    _____ 3. Deciding whether a sample size is large enough to draw conclusions about a population
    _____ 4. Organizing messy data into a clean, usable format
    _____ 5. Calculating measures of center like mean and median
    _____ 6. Recognizing that data represents decisions made by people
    _____ 7. Using correlation and regression to identify relationships between variables
    _____ 8. Evaluating whether claims made in the media are supported by the presented data
    _____ 9. Designing an investigation to collect data that will answer a specific question
    _____ 10. Creating visualizations that are accessible to people with disabilities
    Answer Key: 1-A, 2-E, 3-D, 4-B, 5-C, 6-A, 7-C, 8-D, 9-B, 10-E

    Checkpoint 8. Part 2: Multiple Choice Questions.

    Circle the letter of the best answer for each question.
    1. The data science learning progressions are organized around:
    a) Subject areas like math, science, and social studies
    b) Five interconnected strands that develop over time
    c) Grade-level standards that must be mastered before moving on
    d) Technology tools students need to learn
    2. Which statement best describes how the strands should be taught?
    a) One strand at a time, in alphabetical order
    b) Only the strands that align with your subject area
    c) Integrated together within authentic data investigations
    d) Separately, so students don’t get confused
    3. A kindergarten student sorting objects by color is primarily engaging with:
    a) Advanced statistical analysis
    b) The same concepts as a high school student, just simpler
    c) Foundational ideas that will develop into more sophisticated practices
    d) Skills that are only useful in elementary school
    4. The main difference between elementary and high school expectations in the progressions is:
    a) Elementary students can’t work with real data
    b) High school students use technology while elementary students don’t
    c) The level of complexity, sophistication, and independence expected
    d) Elementary focuses on math while high school focuses on science
    5. When a 6th grader recognizes that a survey might not represent everyone’s opinion, they are demonstrating understanding primarily from:
    a) Strand A (Data Literacy and Responsibility)
    b) Strand B (Creation and Curation)
    c) Strand C (Analysis and Modeling)
    d) Strand D (Interpreting Problems and Results)
    Answer Key: 1-b, 2-c, 3-c, 4-c, 5-d
